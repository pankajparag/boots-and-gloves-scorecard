How to play — Boots & Gloves

Also known as Hand and Foot  ·  Hamanishi rules

Setup

  • Partners or cutthroat: Even number of players → partners; odd → cutthroat
  • Decks: One more than the number of players, jokers included
  • Jokers: One per deck recommended — more jokers means faster, luckier play
  • Who goes first: Each player cuts a card; highest card plays first

The Deal

Each player estimates 22 cards and counts them into two stacks of 11. Pass one stack right, one left. Without looking at the two stacks you received, choose which becomes your glove (hand) and which becomes your boot.

Arrange the cards in your glove from high to low. You are collecting sets of 3 or more of a kind.

Your Turn

  1. Draw 2 cards from the stock pile
  2. Meld or add to your team's existing melds (if eligible)
  3. Discard one card to end your turn

Once at least 7 cards are in the discard pile, you may pick up the top 7 consecutive discards — but only if you can immediately meld the top card of those 7.

Melding (Going Down)

Meld sets of 3 or more of the same rank. Jokers and 2s are wild — max one wild per set. Once one partner melds, the other can add to those same melds on their turn.

Your first meld must meet the minimum for your team's current score:

Books

A stack of 7 or more cards of the same rank is a book. Stack them up — the top card's colour shows the type:

  • 🔴 Red book — no wild cards  +500 pts
  • ⚫ Black book — wild cards allowed  +300 pts

You can continue playing on a completed book or start a new stack for the same rank.

Your Boot

Once you have played all cards in your glove and discarded, pick up your boot. Announce "going into my boot."

If you played every card in your glove without discarding, call "Straight" and pick up your boot — but you cannot go out in the same turn you go straight.

Going Out

To end the round your team needs:

  • All cards played from both your glove and boot
  • At least 1 red book and 1 black book

You may ask your partner "Can I go out?" — they can only answer yes or no (no hints). You may ignore their answer.

Game ends when a team reaches 10,000 pts (partners) or 6,000 pts (cutthroat) at the end of any round.
